CLICKTIONARY

A web-based application for exploring the atoms of object recognition.


Rapid advances in Artificial Intelligence (AI) over recent years have allowed computers to see the world like humans. This has supported a variety of new technologies that change how we live our lives, including Facebook face recognition, Google image search, and self-driving cars. However, we have found that there still exist systematic differences between man and machine:

Different parts of objects are important to humans than AI during object recognition.

In the figure above we demonstrate that humans recognize objects using different information than AI: the glasses frames are important for humans whereas the lenses are important for AI. This means that forcing AI to focus on the same information as humans represents a clear path for driving them significantly closer to human-like recognition.



CLICKTIONARY is a game that provides us with data to bridge the gap between AI and human object recognition.

Every time you play CLICKTIONARY, you provide us with data about what parts of animals and objects humans use for recognition. We use this data in the following ways:

  1. To train AI to act human-like when identifying important image parts.
  2. To measure the extent to which paying attention to the same information as humans during object recognition improves accuracy.

Our early findings, detailed [here] suggest that this approach will significantly improve the performance of AI in object recognition. See below for a graph updated daily showing how AI improves at object recognition when it is forced to pay attention to the same information as humans.



Daily-tracking of AI recognition

Here we record the accuracy of an AI (VGG 16) in recognizing images from a large-scale database (ImageNet). The trace in red is accuracy of a typical VGG 16, and the trace in blue is what happens to its recognition after we force it to pay attention to the same parts of objects as humans.
