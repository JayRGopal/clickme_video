CLICKTIONARY

A web-based application for exploring the atoms of object recognition.


Advances in AI have allowed computers to begin to see the world like humans. In fact, many of the services we use in our daily lives take advantage of these rapid developments, including Facebook face recognition, Google image search, and self-driving cars. However, we have found that there are nevertheless major differences between man and machine:

Modern AI systems recognize objects with different visual features than Humans.

For example, in the figure above we find that when recognizing glasses, leading AIs for computer vision such as the one featured on this website are strongly influenced by the glass lenses. In contrast, recognition for humans is typically based on the glasses frames. We find this difference is systematic, which opens up opportunities to build more human-like AI.

CLICKTIONARY is a game that provides us with data to drive AI towards human-like recognition.

Every time you play CLICKTIONARY, you provide us with valuable data about the parts of animals and objects that Humans use for recognition. We use this data in the following ways:

  • First, to train AIs that will predict these feature locations in any image.
  • Second, to use these predictions to train AIs for more human-like recognition, which we find improves their recognition accuracy.
