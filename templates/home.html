CLICKTIONARY

Game instructions

Welcome to CLICKTIONARY, where you and an artificial intelligence (AI) partner will play to win prizes and provide the research community with valuable information about differences between how humans and machines see the world.

Goal: The goal of the game is to help your AI partner recognize as many object images as possible with as much confidence as possible.

Gameplay: You will help your partner recognize objects by revealing parts of images to it. This is done by "painting" over parts of the object image that help you recognize it. When you click on the image this begins your "brush stroke". After that you simply drag your cursor over the other parts of the image you think are important. These image parts will be revealed to your AI partner as you paint over them. Your AI partner's guesses are shown in the "AI score" graph. The green bar shows its confidence in the correct answer, the red bar in the incorrect answer. As soon as the green bar is greater than the red bar the round ends and you receive points. If the red timer elapses before then you will not receive points.

Scoring: For every image that you and your partner answer correctly you will receive a score of how confident the AI was in that answer. This means that clicking on more "informative" image parts will help your score grow quickly. On the first day of every month we will provide prizes to the top scoring participants. You are automatically assigned a username. If you have not yet given us your email, please enter it below so that we can reward you if you win.

Purpose of the research: You are being asked to participate in a Brown University research study to measure human performance in visual recognition tasks. In this experiment, we seek to gain a basic understanding of how the visual system rapidly interprets and organizes visual scenes.

Inclusion criteria: You must be 18 years or older to participate in this experiment. You must be a native English speaker.

Behavioral Procedures: The task involves viewing images and responding based on instructions you will receive shortly.

Time required: less than 30 minutes.

Benefits: Although there are no direct benefits for the participants in the current study, results may improve our knowledge of the how the brain processes visual objects and scenes.

Compensation: You must complete the entire experiment to receive compensation for your participation.

Participant Risks: There are no anticipated risks for the participants in the current study.

Confidentiality: Your participation in this study is confidential. By signing this document, you permit us to use anonymized information for scientific purposes including teaching and/or publication.

Participation and Withdrawal: You do not have to participate, and you are free to withdraw at any time during the course of the study by closing the browser.
